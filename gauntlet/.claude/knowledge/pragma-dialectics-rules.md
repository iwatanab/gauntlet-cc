# Pragma-Dialectics — Ten Rules and Stage Mapping

Source: Van Eemeren & Grootendorst,
A Systematic Theory of Argumentation (2004)

## Stage 1 — Confrontation

Rule 1 — Freedom rule
Parties must not prevent each other from advancing or questioning standpoints.
Fallacy: ad hominem — attacks the person rather than the argument.
Severity: blocking.

## Stage 2 — Opening

Rule 2 — Burden of proof rule
A party that advances a standpoint is obligated to defend it when asked.
Fallacy: shifting the burden of proof without justification.
Severity: blocking.

Rule 3 — Standpoint rule
Attacks must address the standpoint actually advanced, not a distorted version.
Fallacy: straw man.
Severity: blocking.

## Stage 3 — Argumentation

Rule 4 — Relevance rule
Only argumentation relevant to the standpoint in dispute may be used.
Fallacy: ignoratio elenchi (irrelevant conclusion).
Severity: blocking.

Rule 5 — Unexpressed premise rule
Premises attributed to the other party must be accurate.
Fallacy: false attribution.
Severity: advisory.

Rule 6 — Starting point rule
No premise may be falsely presented as an accepted starting point.
Fallacy: begging the question.
Severity: blocking.

Rule 7 — Argument scheme rule
Schemes must be applied correctly with appropriate CQs answered.
Fallacies: misapplied authority, false analogy, incomplete causal chain.
Severity: blocking.

Rule 8 — Validity rule
Inferences must be logically valid or explicitly flagged as defeasible.
Fallacies: affirming the consequent, denying the antecedent.
Severity: blocking.

## Stage 4 — Concluding

Rule 9 — Closure rule
Failed defences must result in retraction of the standpoint.
Fallacy: refusing to retract a defeated position.
Severity: blocking.

Rule 10 — Usage rule
Language must be clear and unambiguous.
Fallacy: equivocation — exploiting ambiguity to evade scrutiny.
Severity: advisory.

## Opening Stage Checklist

The exchange auditor checks all of the following at the opening stage:
- Has the disagreement been explicitly stated?
- Have shared starting premises been agreed?
- Has the cost of the recommended action been placed on the table
  alongside the risk cited in its favour?
- Has burden_bearer been assigned correctly given dialogue_type?
- Has the burden-bearing party discharged their obligation with the
  current grounds and backing?
